If you're behind, here's what helps.

Nobody wants to hear "you should've started sooner." Here's what you can actually do from where you are, today, next month, next year.

If you're reading this because you think you're behind on retirement saving, a few things are worth knowing up front. First: almost everyone thinks they're behind. You're not alone. Second: where you are now is less important than what you decide to do from here. Third: several things genuinely move the needle. They're worth knowing in the right order.

1. Contribute more, first.

Increasing how much you save is by far the most powerful lever late in the game. More powerful than chasing higher returns, more powerful than tax optimisation, more powerful than re-weighting your portfolio. Ten percent more a month, every month, for the rest of your working life, will reshape your retirement more than most other decisions combined.

2. Use the tax-advantaged vehicles.

An RA gives you tax back today. A TFSA gives you tax-free growth forever. Both are capped, both are free money you're not using if you're not using them. Even an extra R500 a month into one of them starts compounding the moment it's invested.

3. Work a bit longer, if you can.

Delaying retirement by just two or three years has a bigger impact than most people realise. You contribute for longer, your capital compounds for longer, and you have fewer retirement years to fund. Not everyone can. But if the option exists, it's worth weighing honestly.

4. Then, and only then, talk about the portfolio.

Market risk, asset allocation, offshore exposure are all worth getting right. But they're the fine-tuning, not the foundation. Get the saving right first. Then ask whether your portfolio matches your horizon.

You can't undo the years you didn't save. You can decide what the next ten look like. That's a real decision, and it matters.
